    Tubercolosis

    Tuberculosis is a contagious bacterial infection that affects the lungs but can also affect other parts of the body, such as the brain, kidneys, and spine. Symptoms of TB include coughing, chest pain, fatigue, fever, and weight loss. TB can be treated with a combination of antibiotics, but treatment can take several months and requires strict adherence to medication. Without treatment, TB can be deadly.

    Covid 19

    COVID-19, or coronavirus disease 2019, is a highly contagious respiratory illness that has become a global pandemic spreading primarily through respiratory droplets when an infected person talks, coughs, or sneezes. Common symptoms of COVID-19 include fever, cough, fatigue, and difficulty breathing, although some people may have mild or no symptoms. The severity of the disease can range from mild to severe and can be fatal, particularly in older adults or people with underlying health conditions.

    Viral Pneumonia

    Viral pneumonia is a type of pneumonia, which is an infection of the lungs, caused by a viral infection. The symptoms of viral pneumonia can vary but may include cough, fever, difficulty breathing, chest pain, and fatigue. Treatment for viral pneumonia typically involves rest, hydration, and management of symptoms, such as cough suppressants and fever reducers.

    Bacterial pneumonia

    Bacterial pneumonia is a type of pneumonia, which is an infection of the lungs, caused by bacteria. Symptoms of bacterial pneumonia can include cough, fever, difficulty breathing, chest pain, and fatigue. Bacterial pneumonia can be more severe than viral pneumonia and can lead to complications such as sepsis, lung abscesses, and respiratory failure. Treatment for bacterial pneumonia typically involves antibiotics to target the specific bacteria causing the infection, as well as management of symptoms such as fever and cough.
